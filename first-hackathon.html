What to get out of your first hackathon

Talk to sponsors

  • A sponsor is at a hackathon usually to promote their company. They do this through offering information on internships/jobs, offering their insight on how the company is, and explaining how to use their company’s platform in a hack. Take some time to talk with sponsors as most are there because they really want to help hackers and get to know you. I once had a sponsor help debug my code for a project at 3:00am!

Meet other hackers

  • Get to know the other participants! Some of them do some really cool stuff or have great ideas that you could incorporate into your hack. In general most are just really chill to talk to. Be sure to friend them on facebook or follow each other on twitter as you don’t want to lose connections with all your new friends!

Sleep

  • As much as it is advertised that hackathons are not for sleep, be to factor in sleep if you need it. Personally I find that I concentrate and think more clearly when I get a couple hours of sleep versus when I am jacked up on redbull or other caffenated drinks. I had friends that got sick from not sleeping and it affected their schoolwork or jobs the following week. You definitely don’t have to sleep a full 9 hours if you don’t have to, but a getting a good rem cycle in or two goes a long way.

What to bring

  • Here are some useful items to bring that aren’t usally recommended: your hygentic toolkit (toothbrush, toothpaste, deoderant, floss) when it comes down to the final presentation, nobody will want to listen to you when you reak of BO and have morning breathe.
  • Tupperware. Sometimes you might not finish a meal. Sometimes there will be leftovers. Don’t miss out on a free meal!
